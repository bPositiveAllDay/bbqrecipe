How to Grill a Top Sirloin Steak

A nice piece of top sirloin, properly grilled is a sure-fire crowd pleaser. High in protein, and low in sodium, it's good for you as well!

Ingredients

  • 2 1 1/2 inch cuts of top sirloin from your local butcher
  • 2 tablespoons of canola oil
  • Kosher salt, or coarse sea salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper

Directions

Click the pictures as you go to mark your progress.

1. Prep the meat

Season your steaks on both sides, with fresh ground black pepper and your freshly ground sea salt. Do so holding your spices roughly a foot above your meat, to make sure it is applied evenly. Then let the meat sit for 20 minutes, at room temperature. This will ensure the steak is cooked evenly.

2. Prep the grill

Fire up the grill, and turn it to high. Once the grill is good and hot, lightly oil the grates and be careful not to burn yourself! Oiling the grates will enable you to flip the steak without the meat sticking to the grill. It also leaves you with deep, defined grill marks.

3. Grill the meat

Once your steak is at room temperature, throw your meat on the grill. Turn the temperature down to medium-high. For medium-rare, leave it on each side for 5-7 minutes. If you like your steak well done, you can leave it on for a little longer, say 8-13 minutes each side. Remember: Only flip it once!

4.Enjoy!

Once the steak is cooked to your desire, remove from BBQ and let it sit for a couple of minutes. If you're looking for something to pair it with, I recommend serving it with a nice leafy salad, some corn on the cob and some garlic bread!
